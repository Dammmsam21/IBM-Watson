# 07 — PitchBook

## What it is
A **private-capital-markets data and research platform** — the go-to source for **venture capital (VC), private equity (PE), and M&A** information.

## Who owns it / current name
Owned by **Morningstar**. Branded **PitchBook**.

## What you use it for
Researching **private companies, investors, funds, and deals** — who raised money, who invested, valuations, fund performance, and M&A activity that public-market tools cover less well.

## Key features to name in an interview
- Granular **deal data**: financings, rounds, valuations, M&A, exits/IPOs.
- Profiles of **private companies, investors (VC/PE firms), funds, and limited partners**.
- **Cap-table / ownership** and valuation history for private companies.
- Comps, league tables, and benchmarking for private markets.
- Excel plug-in and list/alert tools.

## How *this* role uses it
- **Private-target intelligence:** Identify fast-growing, well-funded private companies that may need (or be switching) auditors.
- **PE-backed targets:** Find companies owned by private-equity firms — these often have audit, transaction, and reporting needs; mapping the PE relationship can open multiple opportunities.
- **Deal triggers:** A funding round, acquisition, or planned IPO is a strong reason for an A&A partner to engage.
- **Investor mapping:** Understand who's behind a target and whether Deloitte has relationships there.

## PitchBook vs. Capital IQ (common question)
Both cover private markets, but **PitchBook is purpose-built for VC/PE/startup deal detail** (rounds, cap tables, startup valuations), while **Capital IQ** is broader financial data with deep public-company and comps strength. Many firms use both as complements.

## Typical tasks / questions it answers
- "Which private companies in fintech raised Series C+ funding in the last year?"
- "Who are the PE owners of this company and what's in their portfolio?"
- "Has this startup signaled an upcoming IPO?"
- "Map the investors and funding history behind Company X."

## Say-this-in-the-interview summary
> "PitchBook, owned by Morningstar, is the leading private-capital-markets database — VC, PE and M&A deals, plus private-company, investor and fund profiles. For audit business development I'd use it to spot well-funded private companies and PE-backed targets that have audit and transaction needs, and to use funding rounds, acquisitions or IPO plans as outreach triggers."
