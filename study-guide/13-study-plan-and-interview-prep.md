# 13 — Study Plan & Interview Prep

## A realistic 5-day study plan

**Day 1 — Frame the role & the buckets**
- Read `00-overview`. Memorize the **four buckets** (News / Firmographics / Financials & Deals / People & Industry).
- Be able to name all nine tools and which bucket each is in.

**Day 2 — Company & news tools**
- Study `01-factiva`, `02-onesource`, `03-dnb-hoovers`.
- Practice: pick a real public company and *describe out loud* how you'd profile it using these three.

**Day 3 — Financials, deals & markets**
- Study `04-thomson-lseg-workspace`, `06-sp-capital-iq`, `07-pitchbook`, `08-bloomberg`.
- Practice the comparisons: **Capital IQ vs. LSEG vs. PitchBook vs. Bloomberg** (see those files).

**Day 4 — People, relationships & industry**
- Study `05-boardex` and `09-ibisworld`.
- Connect BoardEx to the JD's **"cross board analysis"** and **"organization connections."** This is the heart of *Strategic Relationship Management*.

**Day 5 — Delivery + domain + mock interview**
- Study `10-microsoft-office` and `11-core-concepts-and-domain`.
- Do a timed mock using the questions below; review `12-glossary` until the name-changes are automatic.

## Likely interview questions (with how to approach them)

**Tool knowledge**
1. *"Which research databases have you used / are you familiar with?"* — Group by bucket; be honest about exposure level but show you understand each category and can learn fast.
2. *"What's the difference between Capital IQ and PitchBook?"* — Capital IQ = broad public+private financials, comps, screening; PitchBook = private-markets deal granularity (VC/PE/M&A, cap tables).
3. *"You need a quick view of an industry — which tool?"* — IBISWorld (and FirstResearch in D&B Hoovers).
4. *"How would you find a warm introduction to a target CFO?"* — BoardEx relationship mapping.
5. *"Where would you check what's being said about a company?"* — Factiva (news), supported by Bloomberg news.
6. *"You mentioned Hoovers / OneSource / Thomson — are those the current names?"* — Show you know the rebrands (D&B Hoovers; OneSource → D&B; Thomson → LSEG Workspace).

**Scenario / case-style**
7. *"A partner wants to approach a private, PE-backed manufacturer. Walk me through your research."*
   - IBISWorld for the industry → PitchBook/Capital IQ for the company, owner (PE firm) and financials → D&B Hoovers/OneSource for firmographics, family tree and contacts → BoardEx for the CFO/board and a warm-intro path → Factiva for recent news/triggers → synthesize in Excel → storyboard a profile deck + executive summary in PowerPoint/Word.
8. *"How do you decide a company is a good audit target?"* — Look for trigger events: growth, IPO plans, M&A, new CFO, auditor rotation/tenure, restatements.
9. *"How would you build and maintain a targeting database?"* — One clean Excel master table; enrich from multiple sources with XLOOKUP; validate & de-dupe; PivotTables for trends; document source/date; refresh on a cadence (Power Query helps).

**Skills & behavioral (map to the JD wording)**
10. *"Tell me about storyboarding a presentation."* — Situation → insight → so-what → action; action titles; one message per slide; executive summary slide.
11. *"How do you handle ambiguity / tight deadlines / changing priorities?"* — Clarify the ask, prioritize, communicate early, deliver an 80%-right draft fast, then refine on feedback. (The JD literally lists these traits.)
12. *"How do you ensure quality / attention to detail with minimal supervision?"* — Cross-check sources, cite dates, self-review checklist, consistent templates, proof before it reaches a partner.
13. *"Why Deloitte / why Audit & Assurance growth?"* — Tie your research + communication strengths to enabling partners to build client relationships and win work.

## Smart questions YOU can ask the interviewer
- "Which of these databases does the team rely on most day-to-day?"
- "What does a typical research deliverable for a US partner look like?"
- "How is success measured for this role in the first 6–12 months?"
- "How does the USI team collaborate with the US-based Growth team across the time difference?"

## Pre-interview confidence checklist
- [ ] I can name all nine tools and the bucket each belongs to.
- [ ] I know the rebrands (Hoovers→D&B Hoovers, OneSource→D&B, Thomson→LSEG Workspace, Capital IQ→Capital IQ Pro).
- [ ] I can explain, for each tool, *one* thing it's best at.
- [ ] I can compare Capital IQ vs. PitchBook, and Factiva vs. Bloomberg news.
- [ ] I can connect **BoardEx** to "cross board analysis" and warm introductions.
- [ ] I can walk through an end-to-end research-to-deck workflow.
- [ ] I can speak to PowerPoint storyboarding, Excel targeting databases, and Word executive summaries.
- [ ] I understand basic audit/assurance and why trigger events matter for targeting.

## Honest framing for the interview
You will likely *not* have hands-on access to all nine paid platforms before joining — and that's expected. What recruiters want is someone who **understands the landscape, reasons about which tool fits which question, communicates clearly, and learns quickly.** Use this guide to demonstrate exactly that, and be candid about which tools you've actually used versus studied.
