# 05 — BoardEx

## What it is
A **relationship-mapping and people-intelligence platform**. It profiles business leaders and maps the **connections between them** ("who knows who"), all from **publicly available data**.

## Who owns it / current name
BoardEx is part of **Altrata**. Branded **BoardEx**; its concept is often called **Relationship Capital Management**.

## What you use it for
Finding a **warm path** to a decision-maker — i.e., identifying who in your own firm (or network) already has a relationship with a target executive — and researching the **backgrounds of boards and C-suites**.

## Key features to name in an interview
- In-depth profiles of **~1.4–1.8M board members and senior executives** across **~2M+ organizations** (public, private, and not-for-profit), updated frequently.
- Maps **billions of individual connections** (shared employers, board seats, education, clubs, etc.).
- **Relationship paths:** shows how Person A can be introduced to Person B through mutual connections.
- Board summaries: composition, committees, compensation, tenure, diversity ratios.
- **Corporate-events filters** (find leaders present during specific deals) and **lists with daily alerts** on role moves (appointments, departures = outreach triggers).

## How *this* role uses it (this is a *core* tool for "Strategic Relationship Management")
- **Cross-board / connection analysis:** The JD specifically mentions "cross board analysis" and "possible organization connections." BoardEx is exactly the tool for that.
- **Warm introductions:** Find which Deloitte partner shares a connection (former colleague, alma mater, prior board) with a target's CFO or audit-committee chair.
- **Executive backgrounders:** Build bios and board overviews for pursuit materials.
- **Trigger monitoring:** Track executive moves at target accounts to time outreach.

## Typical tasks / questions it answers
- "How is our partner connected to the CFO of Company X?"
- "Who sits on this company's board and audit committee, and what are their backgrounds?"
- "Which of our target executives recently changed roles?"
- "Map the relationship network around this leadership team."

## Say-this-in-the-interview summary
> "BoardEx is a people-intelligence and relationship-mapping platform — profiles of well over a million board members and executives, plus the connections between them, all from public data. For a Strategic Relationship Management role it's central: I'd use it for cross-board analysis, to find warm introduction paths between our partners and a target's decision-makers, and to build executive backgrounders for pursuit materials."
