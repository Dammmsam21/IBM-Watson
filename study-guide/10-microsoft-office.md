# 10 — Microsoft Office (PowerPoint, Excel, Word)

The databases are how you *find* insight. Microsoft Office is how you *deliver* it. The JD asks for **advanced proficiency** and **storyboarding** — in practice, this role lives in **PowerPoint, Excel, and Word**. This is often where you can stand out, because it's fully in your control to master before day one.

---

## PowerPoint — the headline skill

You'll build **executive research decks, target-company profiles, and pursuit materials**. The JD's phrase *"assimilate research findings... to showcase logical thought flow and storyboarding"* is a PowerPoint skill.

**What to master:**
- **Storyboarding** — sketch the *narrative* before building slides: situation → insight → "so what" → recommended action. One key message per slide, stated in the **slide title** (action titles, not labels). *Tip: titles should read like sentences a partner can skim top-to-bottom and understand the whole story.*
- **Slide layouts & hierarchy** — Slide Master, consistent templates, alignment, the "rule" of clean grids; use **guides, gridlines, align/distribute** tools.
- **Data visualization** — choose the right chart (trend = line, composition = stacked bar/100%, comparison = bar, parts-of-whole = use bars not 3D pies). Keep them clean; label directly.
- **Executive summary slide** — one slide that captures the whole story (the JD explicitly wants "executive summaries").
- **Consulting visuals** — simple frameworks, timelines, org/relationship maps, "kicker"/takeaway boxes, harvey balls, traffic-light status.
- **Polish & speed** — Format Painter, Selection Pane, Arrange/Align, reusable templates, brand-compliant colors/fonts, slide sizing (16:9), Tables vs. pasted Excel.

**Interview-ready idea:** *"I build the storyline first, write an action title for every slide, then design the visuals to prove each title — and finish with a one-slide executive summary."*

---

## Excel — analysis + the targeting database

The JD asks you to **"develop and maintain a targeting database," "analyze data, identify trends,"** and do **"cross board analysis."** That's Excel (and sometimes Power BI later).

**What to master:**
- **Data hygiene & structure** — clean, well-structured tables; **Tables (Ctrl+T)**, named ranges, consistent headers, no merged cells in data.
- **Lookups & joins** — `VLOOKUP`/`XLOOKUP`, `INDEX`/`MATCH` (great for merging data pulled from different databases into one targeting sheet).
- **Aggregation & analysis** — **PivotTables & PivotCharts**, `SUMIFS`/`COUNTIFS`/`AVERAGEIFS`, `IF`/`IFS`, `TEXT`, date functions.
- **Cleaning data** — `TRIM`, `LEFT`/`RIGHT`/`MID`, `Text to Columns`, **Remove Duplicates**, Flash Fill, **Power Query** (Get & Transform) for repeatable imports/cleanups.
- **Trend analysis** — growth rates/CAGR, % change, conditional formatting (data bars, color scales, highlight rules), sparklines.
- **Presentation** — clean formatting, number formats, charts that can move into PowerPoint, and dashboards.

**Interview-ready idea:** *"I'd keep one clean master targeting table, enrich it with XLOOKUP from different sources, validate and de-dupe it, then use PivotTables to spot trends and feed charts straight into the deck."*

---

## Word — reports, summaries & communications

Used for **reports, executive summaries, internal/external communications, and marketing collateral**.

**What to master:**
- **Structure & consistency** — **Styles** (Heading 1/2/3), automatic **Table of Contents**, captions, consistent formatting.
- **Professional layout** — headers/footers, page numbers, section breaks, templates, tables.
- **Efficiency & collaboration** — Track Changes, Comments, Compare versions, **Mail Merge** (for personalized outreach/communications).
- **Clear business writing** — concise, structured, audience-appropriate (US stakeholders); strong **executive-summary-first** style (BLUF — "bottom line up front").

**Interview-ready idea:** *"I write bottom-line-up-front, use Word Styles so documents are consistent and easy to navigate, and tailor tone for a senior US audience."*

---

## How the three work together (the deliverable workflow)

1. **Research** in the databases (Sections 01–09).
2. **Excel** — collect, clean, analyze, and store the data (targeting database + trend analysis).
3. **PowerPoint** — storyboard and build the executive research deck/profile.
4. **Word** — write the supporting report, executive summary, or outreach communication.
5. **Review** — proof for accuracy, consistency, and "so-what" before it reaches a partner.

> The JD repeatedly stresses **attention to detail, executive-readiness, and minimal supervision.** Demonstrating a tidy, repeatable Office workflow signals exactly that.
